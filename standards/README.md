# Never Cook Again
## Summary
Our multiplayer party game consists of 1-4 players, who are racing against the clock to prepare a perfectly horrible meal for a restaurant –while ensuring that they don’t get fired before completing it. As the ingredients and recipes come together, players must balance keeping their mission secret from their boss, and see which one can create the most dastardly dish by the end of their shift.

## Player's role
All players in the game are playing as a chef, with the ability to use kitchen appliances and ingredients to make food. There are items around the kitchen that can help create a disgusting food item, the quality of which will determine their score at the end of the game

## Proposed primary gameplay mode
Gameplay will be either 1st person or top-down, dependent on 2D or 3D graphics (TBD). Interactions would mainly be point and click, possibly allowing a small inventory system where the player can pick up items and place them where they need to go. Challenges would include finding the worst ingredients, serving customers to ensure they don’t get fired prematurely, and making sure the boss and other chefs don’t find out what they’re doing. 

## Genre
Our game falls under the genres of simulator, party game, stealth.

## Target audience
Our game rating would likely fall under T (ESRB) as it would contain harsh language, mild deception mechanics, competitive tension and slightly chaotic interactions.

## Target platform
Our game is a Unity game, and might require players to connect to multiplayer in order to play (TBD)

## Competition modes
Multiplayer through machines over a network. We may consider adding local area connection (LAN) or wide area connection (WAN).

## Progression
In the beginning of the game, the player(s) are tasked with performing their roles as a chef, making food for customers. They need to make sure the customers don’t get too dissatisfied with their performance, as complaints may trigger the boss to come check on the player. Meanwhile, the player has to collect nasty ingredients (mold, dead rats, etc) and add them to their nasty dish. Players can sabotage each other by stealing ingredients and trying to get the boss to notice them. Additionally, customers become harder to please as time goes on, making the boss a more constant threat. At the end of the game (5-10 minutes after start) the player serves their masterpiece to the final customer and watches the fireworks. They are then scored by the number of bad ingredients in their dish

## Game world
In the kitchen and dining area of a restaurant.

## Competitive analysis and inspiration
Our #1 competitor is the game Among Us, as it is the same general concept where you vote out an imposter, and allows for many different actions to take place, as well as having the option for more players.
